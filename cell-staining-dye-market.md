<p><ul><li>Acidic Cell Stain</li><li>Basic Cell Stain</li><li>Neutral Cell Stain</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The cell staining dye market is categorized into three main types: acidic, basic, and neutral cell stains. Acidic cell stains, such as eosin, are used to highlight positively charged cellular components, making them suitable for tissue differentiation. Basic cell stains, like hematoxylin, target negatively charged structures, aiding in the visualization of cell nuclei. Neutral cell stains are designed for a balanced approach, effectively highlighting a variety of cellular elements without strong charge bias, facilitating broader applications in research and diagnostics.</p></p>
